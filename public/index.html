About This Project

Project SIBUGAY

A digital glossary dedicated to preserving the Subanen and Kolibugan languages of Zamboanga Sibugay, Philippines. Developed in collaboration with community elders, educators, and researchers to ensure accuracy, cultural sensitivity, and community ownership.

Why It Matters

Indigenous languages in the Philippines are disappearing at an alarming rate. Project SIBUGAY aims to document, teach, and celebrate the linguistic heritage of the Subanen and Kolibugan peoples — not as a museum artifact, but as a living, growing resource for students, elders, and community members.

WMSU · IP-ED Program

This project is supported by the Western Mindanao State University (WMSU) Indigenous Peoples Education (IP-ED) Program, in partnership with the Gukom Council of Elders, local schools, and community leaders across Zamboanga Sibugay.

Ethical Data Use Policy

Community Consent

All entries in this glossary have been reviewed and approved for public sharing by the Gukom Council of Elders. Words marked as "culturally sensitive" contain additional context about their restricted status. No entry is included without the informed consent of community knowledge-keepers.

Attribution & Ownership

Each entry is attributed to its contributor and validator. The cultural knowledge itself remains the intellectual property of the Subanen and Kolibugan communities. This glossary is intended for educational, non-commercial use. Any reproduction or adaptation must be reviewed by the Gukom Council.

Privacy

User accounts store only the information you provide (name, email, role). Activity data (quiz scores, words viewed) is stored locally on your device and is not shared with third parties. We do not track, sell, or monetize user data.

Content Accuracy

We strive for accuracy, but language is living and evolving. If you find an error or have a correction, please use the "Report an issue" feature on the relevant entry. All corrections are reviewed by the editorial team and the Gukom Council.

FPIC (Free, Prior and Informed Consent)

The development of this glossary follows FPIC principles as outlined in the Indigenous Peoples Rights Act (IPRA) of 1997. Community engagement and consent are ongoing processes, not one-time events.

Contact

For questions about data use, content, or community engagement, please reach out through the WMSU IP-ED Program office.
